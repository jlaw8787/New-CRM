# DAY IN THE LIFE TEST: CONSULTANT PERSONA
# Run with browser access against localhost:8080 on seeded data.
# This is NOT a bug hunt. It is a usability and workflow evaluation.
# You are assessing whether a recruiter would WANT to work in this
# tool all day, and what would make them faster.

## Your persona

You are Sarah Keating, a travel nursing recruitment consultant at HCA.
You run a desk of 50-70 nurses. Your KPIs: submissions per week,
placements, speed to fill, and nobody on assignment going dark. You
are busy, interrupted constantly, and have zero patience for clicking
through three screens to do a ten-second job. You have used Bullhorn
and JobAdder at previous agencies, so that is your mental benchmark.

Log in as Sarah Keating and work the following day realistically. Do
each task the way the UI encourages you to, and note every point of
friction as you go.

## The workday

### 8:00 Morning triage (15 min of desk time)
- Open the app. Question one: does the first screen tell you what
  today's work IS? Or do you have to go hunting?
- Work the alerts queue: resolve at least one check-in, one
  compliance item, one follow-up. Count your clicks for each.
- Identify who is going dark on assignment and log a check-in.

### 9:00 Work the hot list
- Review hot candidates. For each: can you tell AT A GLANCE why they
  are hot, when they are available, and what the next action is?
- Pick one hot candidate and find them a role: how do you discover
  which open roles match them? Count the steps.

### 10:00 New candidate intake
- A nurse just called in from a Seek ad. Create her while "on the
  phone": DITL-Test Nurse, ICU RN, QLD, available in 3 weeks.
  Could you keep up if someone were actually talking to you?
- Start her compliance checklist and request first documents.

### 11:00 Submissions push
- Submit two different candidates to roles (full 5-step flow both
  times). Second time through: is the flow fast for a power user, or
  does it make you re-answer things it already knows?
- Update statuses on three older submissions from the facility
  responses you "received by phone": one accepted, one declined, one
  still waiting but chased.

### 1:00 The interruption test
- Mid-task on a candidate profile, a different nurse calls about her
  flight booking. Find that nurse's travel details from cold. How
  many seconds and clicks? Then get back to what you were doing: did
  the app remember where you were?

### 2:00 Compliance chase
- Find every candidate of yours with AHPRA expiring in the next 60
  days without opening profiles one by one. Can you?
- Produce the list you would work from for tomorrow's chase calls.

### 4:00 End of day wrap
- Where do you see what you accomplished today, and what rolls to
  tomorrow?
- If your team leader asked "how is your pipeline looking", what
  screen answers it, and does it answer in numbers a manager wants?

## What to record while working

For EVERY task: clicks required, seconds of hunting, anything you had
to re-type that the system already knew, any info missing at the
moment you needed it, and anything that made you think "Bullhorn does
this better" or "this is better than Bullhorn".

## The report

1. VERDICT: would a real consultant accept working in this daily?
   One honest paragraph.
2. FRICTION LOG: every point of friction found, ordered by how many
   times per day a consultant would hit it (daily pain beats rare
   pain).
3. TOP 10 IMPROVEMENTS: ranked by impact vs build effort. For each:
   what, why, effort (S/M/L), and which daily task it speeds up.
4. WHAT ALREADY WORKS: the things a consultant would genuinely like,
   so we do not accidentally break them chasing improvements.
5. Clean up: list any DITL-Test records created.

Do not fix anything during the run. Report first.
